# Overforged
![overforged-logo.png](src/main/resources/assets/overforged/overforged-logo.png)

### Current Progression:
(every armor requires the previous in the recipe somewhere)

copper -> iron -> iron-steel -> gilded-steel -> diamond -> mercury -> netherite...

copper: regular mining and smelting

iron: have to melt down and forge with original copper armor, iron must be blasted

iron-steel: once obtaining steel ingots, the player must craft a forging basin and forging anvil to begin processing higher level
ores. To start, after iron, you must melt down BOTH iron and steel ingots into molten form, and create an alloy of 
iron-steel by mixing the two in a forging basin. The alloy can be cast into proper iron-steel and after a cooling process,
can be moulded around the original iron armor or tools in a forging anvil, refreshing its durability and upgrading to iron-steel.

gilded-steel: Similar process to iron-steel but instead requires double the steel, alongside molten iron and gold, typically an
expensive upgrade, so you can also use pyrite to create false gilded-steel which is much weaker and has less durability but can
be used to craft pure gilded-steel for a cheaper cost later on.

### Blocklist:

- forging basin [mixing molten metals]
- forging anvil [combining alloy/metal moulds w/ armor/tools]
- casting depot [casting cooled alloys/metals into moulds]
- graphite crucible (cheap) [melting down metals: no blocks/armor pieces, cannot process mercury+]
- silicon-carbide crucible [melting down metals: no limits (for now)]
- molten holding tank [used for storing molten metals, density separation for sorting]
- silicon-carbide launders [can move molten metals over distances]
- carbon-steel pump [helps propel molten metal upwards]

### Itemlist:

- graphite [crafting material]
- silicon [crafting material]
- carbon [crafting material]
- pyrite [crafting/upgrade material]
- steel [crafting/upgrade material]
- mercury [crafting/upgrade material]
